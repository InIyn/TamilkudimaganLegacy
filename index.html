Life & Political Timeline

1938 – Born in Sathanur

M. Tamilkudimagan was born on 15 September 1938 in Sathanur village, Tamil Nadu.

1979–1989 – Principal of Yadav College

Served as Principal of Yadav College, promoting Tamil language, education, and student leadership.

1989 – Speaker of Tamil Nadu Assembly

Released Jayalalitha’s resignation letter, creating political attention.

1996–2001 – Minister for Tamil Culture & Development

Introduced Tamil as a compulsory language in schools and promoted Tamil prayers in temples.

2001 – Shift from DMK to AIADMK

Left DMK due to constituency issues and self-respect; joined AIADMK front to continue serving Tamil Nadu politics.

Full Interview & Political Insights

When Dr M Tamizhkudimagan, Minister for Tamil Culture and Development decided to leave the DMK and join the AIADMK front, it was a political move which created ripples in Tamil Nadu. He, after all, had been with the DMK for 47 years.

Tamizhkudimagan's anger is directed at Chief Minister M Karunanidhi, who had refused him the Elayangudi constituency. He is angrier still because the DMK had decided to allot the seat to a new entrant to the front, the Makkal Tamil Desam floated by a former AIADMK leader, S Kannappan.

As speaker of the state assembly in 1989, Tamizhkudimagan had released the letter from Jayalalitha to the governor of Tamil Nadu expressing her decision to resign as MLA. Later, the issue created a furor in the house.
